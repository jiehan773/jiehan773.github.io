Selected Papers

  • The decision problem for perfect matchings in dense hypergraphs, with Luyining Gan, ICALP 2022, paper 64, 1-16.
    We study a conjecture of Keevash-Knox-Mycroft [STOC '13 & Adv. Math. '15] on the decision problem for hypergraph perfect matchings under minimum l-degree condition. Our result reduces the problem to the study of perfect fractional matchings and thus resolves the algorithmic aspect of the decision problem. As a corollary, we resolve the conjecture for lâ‰¥0.4k.

  • F-factors in quasirandom hypergraphs, with Laihao Ding, Shumin Sun, Guanghui Wang and Wenling Zhou, JLMS, accepted.
    We study the following problem of Lenz and Mubayi: which k-uniform hypergraph F can be perfectly tiled in a sufficiently large linear quasi-random k-uniform hypergraph with a minimum degree condition (assuming the obvious divisibility constraint)? We show that this F-factor problem can be reduced to an F-cover problem, and give a solution to the case k=3.

  • Non-linear Hamilton cycles in linear quasi-random hypergraphs, with Xichao Shu and Guanghui Wang. SODA '21, 74-88.
    We solve a problem of Lenz, Mubayi and Mycroft, showing that given l<k such that k-l does not divide k, linear quasi-randomness together with a minimum (l+1)-degree condition guarantees Hamilton l-cycles in k-uniform hypergraphs, while a similar minimum l-degree does not suffice.

  • On Perfect Matchings in k-complexes, IMRN, 11(2021), 8741â€“8762.
    We give new proofs of several characterization theorems for the existence of perfect matchings in dense simplicial complexes by Keevash and Mycroft [Mem. AMS, 2015]. In particular, our proof avoids the use of the hypergraph regularity lemma and the hypergraph blow-up lemma. Instead, we use the lattice-based absorbing method developed by Han and a recent probabilistic argument of Kohayakawa, Person and Han.

  • Decision problem for Perfect Matchings in Dense k-uniform Hypergraphs, Trans. AMS, 369-7(2017), 5197-5218.
    We show that the decision problem for the containment of perfect matchings in k-uniform hypergraphs with minimum codegree at least n/k can be solved in polynomial time. This solves a problem of Karpinski, Rucinski and Szymanska completely and improves the work of Keevash, Knox and Mycroft.
